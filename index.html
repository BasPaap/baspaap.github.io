Interactive campus map
With customizable content

An map of the TU Delft campus with realtime per-building news, tweets, public transport information, weather, traffic, flocking birds, flying rockets and a surprise robot! The application ran on a 100" touchscreen in the campus central library, allowing users to find faculties, check faculty calendars, and more.

The client application was built in Unity3D using a variety of first and third party REST API's to make the map come alive. Information displayed on the map could be updated via a CMS built on ASP.NET MVC in C#.

Intuitive voting
just by lifting a finger

An experiment in intuitive, seamless interaction. For a library's book of the week competition, two contenders were placed in a display case featuring a transparent display, instructing passers by to point at the book they wished to nominate. A LEAP Motion detector hidden in the base detected the pointing motion and determined the selected book, upon which the non-contender got obscured by the darkened display and the vote count got updated.

Built in WPF 4.5 on a Samsung transparent display running Windows Embedded, using the LEAP Motion API.

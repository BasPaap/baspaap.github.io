De Poorten van Alexandria

Around the turn of the 19th century, magic has all but left our world. On a Dutch sailing ship just off the coast of Egypt, an eclectic group of scientists, artists, occultist, adventurers and the odd stowaway are caught up in the struggle between various organizations who wish to seize upon the last bit of supernatural power that remains - at any cost.

De Poorten van Alexandria (The Gates of Alexandria) is an annual series of role playing events taking place on a 19th century sailing ship cruising the IJsselmeer - doubling for the Mediterranean. It is about loss and rebirth, about magic and science. For this event I am co-organizing, co-writing characters and the scenario, creating visuals, building props and have developed an interactive dream in virtual reality for several "lucky" passengers.

Syndicaat

The big city, 1930's America. Prohibition reigns - and so does organized crime. In the various speakeasies and gambling dens, the major crime families meet and mingle with corruptible politicians, extravagant movie stars and reporters always on the hunt for the next story. In a place where life is cheap and profits are big, is blood really thicker than water?

Syndicaat (Syndicate) is an annual series of role playing events set in crimetown USA, in the age of prohibition and organized crime. The concept of family runs through everything, but it is up to the players to decide where their loyalties lie and whom they consider to be family. I'm co-organizing, co-writing characters and plot, creating visuals and building props

and scenery for this event. For the second edition set at a race track, I built an interactive horse racing simulator to facilitate gambling and match fixing for the players.

18 uur

When society is collapsing, what makes one human? Where does civilization make way for survival instincts? And who keeps their heads cool when faced with a choice between commiting atrocities or sacrificing themselves for the good of others? Did I mention there's also a zombie apocalypse going on?

18 uur ran in February of 2019 and is set to re-run in 2020. I co-organized and made a variety of props, visuals, and special effects - including lots of gore and several liters of blood - for this cinematic rollercoaster experience.

Wolfhagen

In the mid-eighteenth century, in what is now the southernmost tip of the Netherlands, a small village is suffering from heavy taxation, brutal repression by the church, and years of warfare. To make matters worse, the Goat Riders, the legendary gang of devil-worshipping brigands - have been seen riding across the night sky, ready to make their return.

Wolfhagen was a historical role playing event about poverty, politics, family and superstition - or was it all true after all? I have co-written characters and plot, built props and sets and co-organized this event with various others.

Interactive campus map
With customizable content

An interactive map of the TU Delft campus with realtime per-building news, tweets, public transport information, weather, traffic, flocking birds, flying rockets and a surprise robot! The application ran on a 100" touchscreen in the campus central library, allowing users to find faculties, check faculty calendars, and more.

I built the client application in Unity3D using a variety of first and third party REST API's to make the map come alive. Information displayed on the map could be updated via a CMS, which I built on ASP.NET MVC in C#.

Intuitive voting
just by lifting a finger

An experiment in intuitive, seamless interaction. For a library's book of the week competition, two contenders were placed in a display case featuring a transparent display, instructing passers by to point at the book they wished to nominate, upon which the winning book's vote count was updated, and the display darkened to obscure the other title.

I built the user interface in WPF 4.5 to run on a Samsung transparent display running Windows Embedded, and used the Leap Motion API to handle motion tracking.

Classical masterpieces
come alive in 3D

An experiment in getting schoolchildren engaded with art via virtual reality. A spotlight follows the user's gaze in a museum of classical art, allowing for intuitive interaction without distractions. Works of art can be brought up close to the user, coming alive with the sound of angelic voices, gunfire, murmuring crowds and more.

I built this experiment in Unity3D to run on Google Cardboard, using a variety of API's were used to obtain high-res images of thousands of classical masterpieces.

Visual storytelling
made easy

A big, bright, attractive application that unlocks the treasure trove of digitized heritage in any library, archive or museum. Working with a variety of media, curators can easily click together a story told with photographs, audio, video, tweets, text and more, putting it directly under the fingertips of visitors and educators.

The client was built in WPF 4.5 and the Microsoft Surface 2 Framework, using a variety of social media API's to display, for example, twitter feeds. An easy to use CMS was built on ASP.NET MVC in C#, and used a variety of REST and OAI-PMH API's to obtain content from various sources. Touch tables running this application are currently found in libraries across Europe and North America.

Eurovision scoreboard
for realtime score visualisation

For a real-life gaming experience based on the Eurovision Song Contest, this scoreboard application collected every nation's votes and displayed them on stage, using striking visuals and animations.

The application was built in WPF 4.5 and allowed for interactive, real-time voting mimicking the traditional Eurovision televoting ceremony. Get the code here!
